The hgroup element can contain only h1,...,h6 elements. Here is an example:

h1 and h2

10 Terrific Tech Blogs by Women

Code, Gadgets, Issues, and Science

It isn’t necessary to use an h1/h2 combination. It could be an h2/h3 or h3/h4. It
depends on where you’re putting it. The defining attribute of hgroup elements is that
the first heading element is what goes into the document outline. The secondary heading
is not part of the outline. The secondary headings in the hgroup are meant for taglines,
subheads, and other lesser text that relates to the main heading in the hgroup.

h1 and h3

10 Terrific Tech Blogs by Women

Code, Gadgets, Issues, and Science

h3 and h4

10 Terrific Tech Blogs by Women

Code, Gadgets, Issues, and Science

h2 and h6

10 Terrific Tech Blogs by Women

Code, Gadgets, Issues, and Science
